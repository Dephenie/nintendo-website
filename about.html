History of Nintendo

From playing cards to global gaming giant

Nintendo was founded in 1889 by Fusajiro Yamauchi in Kyoto, Japan. The company originally made traditional Japanese playing cards called Hanafuda. For a long time, that's all they did.

In the 1970s, Nintendo started experimenting with electronics and eventually entered the video game market. Their early arcade games like Donkey Kong helped them get noticed internationally, especially in the US.

TheNES (Famicom)launched in 1983 and basically saved the video game industry after the 1983 crash. Games like Super Mario Bros. and The Legend of Zelda became iconic and set the standard for what video games could be.

Since then, Nintendo has kept releasing new consoles — Game Boy, SNES, N64, GameCube, Wii, DS, 3DS, and eventually the Nintendo Switch in 2017. They've always focused on making games fun rather than just chasing the best graphics.
